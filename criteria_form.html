Procurement case I of III
💊 Gliclazide
Case details
Gliclazide is a second-generation sulfonylurea used primarily in the management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM). It is distinguished within its class by its partial selectivity for pancreatic sulfonylurea receptors and its perceived cardiovascular safety profile. This drug was shown to have high utilization and expenditure and are a drug used in diabetes, the top 2 utilised therapeutic subgroup in 219-2022.
Procurement case II of III
💊 Metformin
Case details
Metformin Hydrochloride is a biguanide oral antidiabetic agent and the first-line pharmacotherapy for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM). It is one of the highest-volume medications procured across MOHE facilities, with a large number of registered generic alternatives in the Malaysian market. Gastrointestinal tolerability and excipient profile are key differentiators between generics, and formulation type — immediate-release versus extended-release — must be carefully matched during procurement.
Procurement case III of III
💊 Atorvastatin
Case details
Atorvastatin Calcium is an HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor (statin) indicated for primary hyperlipidaemia, mixed dyslipidaemia, and cardiovascular risk reduction. It is a high-volume medication with significant long-term cardiovascular impact across the patient population. Multiple generic alternatives are registered in the Malaysian market, with variability in bioequivalence data quality between manufacturers. Stability, storage conditions, and packaging quality are important considerations given the lipophilic nature of the compound and its role in long-term adherence.
