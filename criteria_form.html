Definition of terms
  • Off-patent pharmaceuticals (OPP): Medications with no active patents
  • Multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA): A structured, systematic framework used to evaluate multiple, often conflicting options against a set of predefined criteria.
  • Mandatory criteria: Binary specification / criteria to fulfil and perform positively to meet the minimum standard in order to be considered for evaluation.
  • MCDA criteria: Performance-based specification / criteria used to score and rank the eligible alternatives through weighted trade-offs.
Conceptual Framework for Tender Workflow Integration

The framework resulting from this research is designed for direct integration into the Penilaian Teknikal and Penilaian Kewangan phase of the current tender workflow in USM-affiliated healthcare facilities. By mapping scientifically rigorous methodology onto existing procurement process flows, the resulting framework facilitates a seamless transition from conceptual design to practical implementation to enhance the reproducibility of value-based outcomes.

While the framework facilitates an integrated assessment of financial and technical criteria, its modular construct allows the specific committees to evaluate their respective domains independently. Ultimately, the resulting data subsequently merges into a unified, multi-dimensional and consolidated evaluation report.

What is MCDA?

Making a complex decision involves more than just looking at a single factor like the lowest price. Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) is a structured framework used to compare different options by evaluating several factors simultaneously. Consider it as a professional "balancing act." Instead of choosing a medicine based solely on one criteria (i.e. price or efficacy), MCDA allows for the consideration of relevant criteria at the appropriate weightage. It transforms subjective opinions into a clear, transparent, and data-driven choice.

Why Use MCDA for OPP Procurement?

In a healthcare environment, the "best" drug is rarely defined by one characteristic. There are various applications of MCDA in pharmaceutical decision making such as drug listing that focuses on therapeutic need — where cost effectiveness and clinical superiority is highlighted. Procurement must balance competing priorities to ensure the highest standard of care. MCDA's role in drug procurement is critical and unique because the supply resilience and value is evaluated to justify 'Value for Money' for drug procurement. By using this method, the procurement process becomes repeatable, defensible, and focused on delivering the best overall value to both the patient and the institution.

Procurement Case I of III
💊 Gliclazide
Gliclazide is a second-generation sulfonylurea used primarily in the management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM). It is distinguished within its class by its partial selectivity for pancreatic sulfonylurea receptors and its perceived cardiovascular safety profile.

This drug belongs to the top 2 utilised therapeutic subgroup and was shown to have high utilization and expenditure in 2019–2022. A total of 36 products is registered across three strengths: 30mg (10 products), 60mg (10 products), 80mg (16 products). They are available in multiple oral formulations, including both immediate-release and modified-release dosage forms.
Procurement Case II of III
💊 Ceftriaxone
Ceftriaxone is utilized as a broad-spectrum, once-daily parenteral agent for the treatment of community-acquired pneumonia, complicated urinary tract infections, and as a common choice for surgical prophylaxis.

It was shown to have the highest utilisation in both all wards and ICU categories from 2008 till 2017. A total of 28 products is registered across three strengths: 1g (15 products), 500mg (9 products), and 250mg (4 products).
Procurement Case III of III
💊 Levothyroxine
Levothyroxine sodium is a synthetic form of thyroxine T4 used as first-line replacement therapy for hypothyroidism to restore normal metabolic function. Due to its Narrow Therapeutic Index (NTI), brand interchangeability is limited.

The utilisation of levothyroxine in the public sectors increased by 37% in 2022 compared to 2017. A total of 8 products is available across three strengths: 25mcg (2 products), 50mcg (3 products), 100mcg (3 products).
