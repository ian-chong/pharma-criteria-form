Introduction
Modified Delphi questionnaire

A modified Delphi panel is a structured, iterative, and anonymous consensus-building technique that begins with pre-selected evidence-based items.

This is Round 1 of 3 consecutive rounds. In this Round 1, you will be expected to assess the decision problem, criteria, definition and performance levels related to three procurement cases. The same set of questions will be repeated across three procurement cases. Please evaluate each case independently based on your professional experience and knowledge.

Definition of terms
  • Off-patent pharmaceuticals (OPP): Medications with no active patents
  • Multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA): A structured, systematic framework used to evaluate multiple, often conflicting options against a set of predefined criteria.
  • Mandatory criteria: Binary specification / criteria to fulfil and perform positively to meet the minimum standard in order to be considered for evaluation.
  • MCDA criteria: Performance-based specification / criteria used to score and rank the eligible alternatives through weighted trade-offs.

Procurement Case I of III
💊 Gliclazide
Gliclazide is a second-generation sulfonylurea used primarily in the management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM). It is distinguished within its class by its partial selectivity for pancreatic sulfonylurea receptors and its perceived cardiovascular safety profile.
  • Dose: 30mg, 60mg, 80mg
  • Dosage available oral immediate release, modified release
  • This drug belongs to the top 2 utilised therapeutic subgroup and was shown to have high utilization and expenditure in 2019–2022.
Procurement Case II of III
💊 Metformin
Metformin Hydrochloride is a biguanide oral antidiabetic agent and the first-line pharmacotherapy for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM). It is one of the highest-volume medications procured across MOHE facilities, with a large number of registered generic alternatives in the Malaysian market. Gastrointestinal tolerability and excipient profile are key differentiators between generics, and formulation type — immediate-release versus extended-release — must be carefully matched during procurement.
Procurement Case III of III
💊 Atorvastatin
Atorvastatin Calcium is an HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor (statin) indicated for primary hyperlipidaemia, mixed dyslipidaemia, and cardiovascular risk reduction. It is a high-volume medication with significant long-term cardiovascular impact across the patient population. Multiple generic alternatives are registered in the Malaysian market, with variability in bioequivalence data quality between manufacturers. Stability, storage conditions, and packaging quality are important considerations given the lipophilic nature of the compound and its role in long-term adherence.
